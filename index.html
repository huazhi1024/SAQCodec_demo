SAQCodec: Achieving High Signal-level and Application-level Performance in Ultra-Low Bitrates

Abstract

We propose SAQCodec, a semantic-aware and highly efficient neural speech codec designed for ultra-low bitrate scenarios down to 0.3kbps. Our method introduces a semantic-aware encoder that fuses acoustic and contextual representations, an Adaptive Progressive Residual Vector Quantization (APRVQ) module for a coarse-to-fine quantization with enhanced efficiency, and a Conditional Entropy Quantization (CEQ) loss to encourage structured codebook usage. Comprehensive experiments demonstrate that SAQCodec outperforms existing baselines in both signal-level and application-level metrics, while maintaining low model complexity and fast inference. Notably, SAQCodec achieves competitive or superior performance even compared to 1 kbps codecs, despite operating at significantly lower bitrates. Ablation results confirm the effectiveness of proposed components. These findings suggest that SAQCodec offers a practical and generalizable solution for speech coding in bandwidth-constrained conditions.
